Problem 3¶

We need to procure 3000 lb. of cashews a week from the local market.

There are a couple of key considerations here:

  1. Does the market sell out everyday?
  2. What is the cost of one trip to the market?
  3. What is the carrying capacity of our truck to the market, how does this scale in regards to more trucks?
  4. How soon are these cashews distributed further?
    • Where are the cashews stored prior to further distribution?
  5. What is the storage capacity of this distribution center?
    • How long can cashews be effectively stored?
    • How does the cost of storage scale?
  6. How reliable are the market deliveries?
    • Are the cashews consistently of good quality year-round, or are some days/time periods better than others?
    • Where are they sourced from? How do delays in that supply chain affect the market deliveries?
  7. How do the market prices change over time? Are they subject to supply/demand shocks and falls?
  8. How much of the total supply to the market can we capture?
  9. Are there are any other potential channels that could be tapped?

Since the max of 2 years of data is 2717 lbs and we can see from above that there is a fair amount of variation, with some days being particularly bad:

  • Cannot satisfy 3000 lb. weekly need with just one weekly trip to the market
  • Problem becomes - how often to go to the market then, as to minimize trips but maximize amount of cashews obtained
  • Therefore we look at the seasonality in these deliveries, are they completely random or is there a general pattern
    • e.g. Every Monday in general has more lbs. available than any given Tuesday, on average
    • If we can discover such patterns we can optimize our trips around these days

# Simple Prophet model to extract seasonality
df1 = df[['Date','net_weight']]
df1 = df1[df1['net_weight'] > 2000] # Removing outliers
df1 = df1.rename(columns={df1.columns[0]: 'ds', df1.columns[1]: 'y'})
model = Prophet().fit(df1)
future = model.make_future_dataframe(periods=30, freq='D')
forecast = model.predict(future)
fig = model.plot(forecast)
fig2 = model.plot_components(forecast)
